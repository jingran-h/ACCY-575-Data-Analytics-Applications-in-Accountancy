# Module 19 — Financial Communications: MD&A Disclosure Quality

> **The engagement:** [brief.md](brief.md) — a disclosure-review mandate on a real 10-K. How you assess it is yours.

## Motivation

The third engagement moves from the numbers to the words. Before a 10-K is signed, a disclosure committee has to satisfy itself that the MD&A is a complete and balanced account of the business — not a polished one that quietly drops the things a reader needs. You are asked to benchmark a real filed MD&A against its peers and against the firm's own results, and to flag where the disclosure looks thin or one-sided for counsel to revisit.

The question — *is this disclosure adequate?* — is genuinely open, and so is your approach. You have a corpus of real filings and the full text-analysis toolkit from Part 2; what you measure, and how, is your judgment to make and defend.

## Goals

By the end of this engagement you will have:

- Turned an open-ended disclosure-quality question into a concrete, evidence-backed review — deciding for yourself what "adequate" means and how to measure it.
- Directed the agent to build the analysis over real 10-K text, and validated its outputs before trusting them.
- Benchmarked a firm's narrative against peers and against its own numbers, and told the two apart.
- Produced a disclosure-committee briefing and redline suggestions a general counsel's office could act on.

## What you deliver

A disclosure-committee briefing and a set of redline suggestions flagged for counsel, backed by a reproducible repo. Full spec in the [engagement brief](brief.md); grading in [Assessment across Part 3](../README.md#assessment-across-part-3).

## Going Deeper *(optional)*

*The launch briefing frames the engagement. These are references you may consult as you would on the job, not required reading.*

- **SEC Regulation S-K, Item 303** (and the SEC's MD&A interpretive guidance) — what an MD&A is actually required to cover: results of operations, liquidity, and known trends and uncertainties. The standard your review is measured against.
- **The SEC's plain-English and disclosure-effectiveness guidance** — the regulator's own view of what makes a disclosure clear versus evasive.
