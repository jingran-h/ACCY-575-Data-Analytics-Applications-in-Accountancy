# Module 20 — Advisory Capstone: Acquisition Screening & Diligence

> **The engagement:** [brief.md](brief.md) — a two-sentence client mandate. Your team scopes everything else and defends it live.

## Motivation

The capstone is the whole course in one engagement. A client hands your advisory team a two-sentence mandate — go find us an acquisition — and everything after that is yours: the universe, the screen, the definition of a good target, the diligence, and the recommendation. Then you defend it to an investment committee that will push back on every choice.

This is the closest the course gets to real advisory work. There is no method to follow and no answer key. You have the full Part 1–2 toolkit, real market data, and a team; the deliverable is a recommendation someone could act on and a defense that survives the room.

## Goals

By the end of this engagement your team will have:

- Scoped an open client mandate into a defensible screen and diligence program — every criterion your own choice, every choice interrogable.
- Directed the agent to build the end-to-end pipeline across financial and filing data, and caught where its clean-looking output rests on artifacts of the data.
- Separated a target that is genuinely attractive from one that only looks that way on a screen.
- Produced an investment-committee report and deck, and defended the recommendation live.

## What you deliver

An investment-committee advisory report and deck with a meet/pass recommendation, defended live, backed by a reproducible team repo. Full spec in the [engagement brief](brief.md); grading in [Assessment across Part 3](../README.md#assessment-across-part-3).

## Going Deeper *(optional)*

*The launch briefing frames the engagement. These are references you may consult as you would on the job, not required reading.*

- **Earnings quality and accruals** (Sloan 1996; the Beneish M-score) — how analysts flag financials that may not mean what they appear to. Optional background for the diligence read.
- **Working-capital and cash-conversion diligence** — any financial-statement-analysis text: DSO, DIO, DPO, and the cash conversion cycle, and what their trends reveal.
