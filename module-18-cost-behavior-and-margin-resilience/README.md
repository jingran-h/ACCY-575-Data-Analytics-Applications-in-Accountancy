# Module 18 — Managerial Accounting: Cost Behavior & Margin Resilience

> **The engagement:** [brief.md](brief.md) — a CFO's question and a database of real firms' filed financials. The method is yours.

## Motivation

The second engagement puts you in the controller's seat. The CFO asks a blunt planning question — *what happens to our margin if revenue falls?* — and hands you the raw financial history to answer it. Finance's first-cut answer (assume cost falls in proportion to sales) is usually wrong, because much of a firm's cost is committed capacity that does not leave when volume dips. Turning that intuition into a number the controller can defend is the work.

As in every Part 3 engagement, nobody hands you a procedure. You have the Part 1–2 toolkit and a real dataset; how you estimate cost behavior and translate it into a margin forecast is your call.

## Goals

By the end of this engagement you will have:

- Turned an open CFO planning question into a concrete, estimable analysis on real financial-statement data — choosing the approach yourself.
- Directed the agent to build the estimation and the scenario, and judged where its output is fragile or extrapolated past what the data supports.
- Distinguished cost that genuinely comes out in a downturn from cost that is committed and stays.
- Produced a decision memo and a board-ready exhibit a CFO could take into an operating review.

## What you deliver

A controller/CFO decision memo and a one-page board exhibit on margin resilience, backed by a reproducible repo. Full spec in the [engagement brief](brief.md); grading in [Assessment across Part 3](../README.md#assessment-across-part-3).
